Research

My research explores galaxy evolution through observational astronomy, using advanced telescopes to study galaxy dynamics across cosmic time.

Ultra-Diffuse Galaxies

My work on ultra-diffuse galaxies (UDGs) has revealed:

  • Gas-rich UDGs deviate from the baryonic Tully-Fisher relation
  • These galaxies have unusually low dark matter densities
  • They may have baryon fractions approaching cosmological limits
Read the Paper

Angular Momentum in Galaxies

Key discoveries about angular momentum:

  • Detailed measurements of specific angular momentum across galaxy masses
  • Tight relation between mass, angular momentum, and gas fraction
  • Important constraints for galaxy formation models
Read the Paper

Gas Scale Heights

My work on gas discs has shown:

  • Gas discs are thicker than often assumed
  • Discs flare with radius due to pressure-gravity balance
  • New method for estimating flaring and mass models
Read the Paper
